1. Analysis of the text “Money as a means or an end” (Aristotle and Epicurus)

Money was created as a means to facilitate exchanges between people. For Aristotle, its purpose was to serve a good life, not dominate it. Using money as a means means seeing it as a tool to satisfy real needs such as food, shelter, education or rest. When money becomes an end, it controls our lives, creates meaningless desires and distances us from what matters. Aristotle values virtue and the good life above money. Epicurus believes true happiness comes from fulfilling basic needs, cultivating friendship, serenity and thought.

Answers:
a) Its original function was to be a means of exchange.
b) As a means, money is a tool; as an end, it becomes an obsession.
c) Consequences: dependence, anxiety, loss of freedom, empty desires.
d) Aristotle values virtue; Epicurus values friendship, serenity and thought.
e) Happiness comes from simplicity and essential needs.

2. Reflection: To consume or to be

We live surrounded by messages telling us what to buy and how to live. Advertising and social networks influence our desires. Consuming does not always mean living better. Fromm distinguishes “having” from “being”: identity based on possessions creates dependence, while being leads to deeper happiness. Conscious consumption means choosing freely what truly benefits us.

Answers:
a) Needs are essential; desires are often created by marketing.
b) Money gives freedom when used wisely, but dependence when it controls us.
c) “The role of money in my life”: Money is a tool to satisfy real needs, but it should not define who I am.

3. Ethics of consumption and responsibility (Class 2)

Responsible consumers think before acting: questioning if we really need what we want, considering how products are made, and thinking about the consequences our decisions have on society and the planet. Conscious consumption is not about stopping enjoyment but choosing with meaning.

Answers:
1. We are not fully free, since advertising influences us.
2. Conscious consumption means thinking before buying and considering impact.
3. Yes, we can be happier with fewer things.
4. Concept map content: need vs desire, impact, responsibility, ethics, Kant, Fromm, Epicurus.
